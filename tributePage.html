About


Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 to a Tamil Muslim family in the pilgrimage centre of Rameswaram on Pamban Island, then in the Madras Presidency and now in the State of Tamil Nadu. His father Jainulabdeen was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque; his mother Ashiamma was a housewife.His father owned a ferry that took Hindu pilgrims back and forth between Rameswaram and the now uninhabited Dhanushkodi.Kalam was the youngest of four brothers and one sister in his family.His ancestors had been wealthy traders and landowners, with numerous properties and large tracts of land. Their business had involved trading groceries between the mainland and the island and to and from Sri Lanka, as well as ferrying pilgrims between the mainland and Pamban. As a result, the family acquired the title of "Mara Kalam Iyakkivar" (wooden boat steerers), which over the years became shortened to "Marakier." With the opening of the Pamban Bridge to the mainland in 1914, however, the businesses failed and the family fortune and properties were lost over time, apart from the ancestral home.By his early childhood, Kalam's family had become poor; at an early age, he sold newspapers to supplement his family's income.

Education

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was a person who loved learning and education. He went to school in Ramanathapuram, Tamil Nadu, India, and then went on to study physics at St. Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli, India. He later completed a Master's degree in Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology in Chennai. He received an honorary doctorate degree from 40 universities around the world, including the University of Wolverhampton in the UK and the University of Waterloo in Canada. Dr. Kalam also underwent training at several institutions, including NASA in the USA and DRDO in India. He believed in the power of education and encouraged young people to pursue careers in science and technology.

Career


Dr. Kalam's career began as a scientist at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) in India. He played a vital role in the development of India's missile program, and his contributions to the successful testing of the Agni and Prithvi missiles are well known.

Dr. Kalam was elected as the President of India in 2002, serving in this role until 2007. During his presidency, he focused on youth development, education, and scientific research, earning him widespread respect and admiration.


Dr. Kalam was also a prolific writer and author, with many of his books aimed at inspiring and motivating young people. In addition, he was a passionate teacher and mentor, teaching at various institutions, including the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) and the Banaras Hindu University.

Personal Life

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam had a simple and modest personal life. He was born into a middle-class Tamil Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He did not get married and did not have any children. He enjoyed reading books, writing poetry, and playing musical instruments like the Veena and the tabla. Dr. Kalam was a vegetarian and believed in simple and healthy living. He was a devout Muslim and regularly prayed and read the Quran. He was known for his inspiring personality, simplicity, and dedication to serving the country.

Awards

1.Padma Bhushan (1981): This is one of the highest civilian awards in India, given by the Government of India for distinguished service of a high order in any field.

2.Padma Vibhushan (1990): This is the second-highest civilian award in India, given by the Government of India for exceptional and distinguished service to the nation in any field.

3.Bharat Ratna (1997): This is the highest civilian award in India, given by the Government of India for exceptional service towards the advancement of art, literature, and science, and in recognition of public service of the highest order.

4.Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration (1997): This award was given by the Government of India for promoting national integration.

5.Ramanujan Award (2000): This award was given by the Alwars Research Centre, Chennai, for his outstanding contributions to the field of science.

6.King Charles II Medal (2007): This award was given by the Royal Society, UK, for his outstanding contribution to science and engineering.

7.Hoover Medal (2008): This award was given by the ASME Foundation, USA, for his outstanding public service and contribution to engineering.

8.International von Karman Wings Award (2009): This award was given by the California Institute of Technology, USA, for his contribution to the fields of science and technology.

9.Honorary Doctorate Degrees: He received numerous honorary doctorate degrees from universities around the world, including Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, University of Waterloo, Canada, and University of Wolverhampton, UK.

10.The A.P.J. Abdul Kalam IGNITE Awards: The National Innovation Foundation, India, has established these awards for school students to encourage creativity and originality in science and technology.
